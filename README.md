# Canadian Housing Market Analysis

A Python-based exploratory data analysis comparing new housing price trends
across 5 Canadian cities from 1981 to 2026, with a focus on how St. John's,
Newfoundland and Labrador compares to the national market.

## Project Overview

As someone living in St. John's and considering buying a home, I wanted to
understand where NL sits in the broader Canadian housing landscape. This
project uses 45 years of Statistics Canada data to tell that story through
clean visualizations and data analysis.

## Key Findings

- St. John's has consistently been one of the most affordable housing markets
  in Canada over the past 45 years
- The post-2020 pandemic boom hit every city hard, but St. John's saw the most
  modest increase of all five cities analyzed
- Despite 204% total growth since 1981, St. John's remains well below the
  national average today
- Calgary showed the highest total growth at 400%+ but started from a very low base
